1. Summary

DriveCam is a camera recording utility that lets users record videos, capture photos, save media locally on the device or selected storage folders, and optionally upload media to the user's own Google Drive. DriveCam is designed to keep user recordings under the user's control.

DriveCam does not sell personal information. DriveCam does not use personal information for third-party advertising. DriveCam only accesses Google account and Google Drive data to provide sign-in, Drive storage status, upload, playback, folder management, and deletion features requested by the user.

2. Information We Access or Collect

The information DriveCam accesses or collects depends on the permissions you grant and the features you use.

3. Permissions Used by DriveCam

  • Camera: required to show preview, record video, capture photos, switch front/back cameras, and zoom.
  • Microphone: required to record audio with videos.
  • Location: used only when location-related media details are enabled and permission is granted.
  • Notifications: used to show recording, upload, incomplete upload, and background task status where supported.
  • Storage / media / folder access: used to save recordings locally, save to a selected folder, create DriveCam folders, and manage temporary files.
  • Network access: used for Google sign-in, Google Drive upload/playback, Drive storage status, and network speed tests.

4. How We Use Information

DriveCam uses information only to provide and improve user-facing app features, including:

  • recording videos and capturing photos;
  • saving files to local storage or a user-selected storage folder;
  • uploading videos and photos to the user's Google Drive when Drive is selected;
  • creating and organizing DriveCam folders by media type and date;
  • showing upload progress, upload speed, recording duration, and upload history;
  • resuming or retrying interrupted uploads where possible;
  • displaying Drive storage usage, device storage usage, and low-storage warnings;
  • listing DriveCam date folders and playing recorded videos from the app;
  • applying user-selected deletion schedules for DriveCam folders; and
  • performing connection latency and speed tests.

5. Google Sign-In and Google Drive

DriveCam uses Google Sign-In and Google Drive APIs so that users can upload recordings to their own Google Drive and manage files created by DriveCam. When you sign in, DriveCam may access your Google profile information, such as name, email address, and profile photo, to display the active account in the app.

DriveCam uses Google Drive access to create and manage DriveCam folders, upload files selected or created by the app, display Drive storage quota, list DriveCam media folders, stream videos for playback, and delete DriveCam folders or files when the user configures deletion features.

DriveCam's use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. DriveCam does not use Google user data for advertising, does not sell Google user data, and does not allow humans to read Google user data except where necessary for security, legal compliance, or with the user's explicit consent.

6. Local Storage and User-Selected Folders

If you choose internal or local storage, DriveCam saves recordings on the device or in a storage folder selected by you. DriveCam may create folders such as:

  • DriveCam/Videos/yyyy-MM-dd
  • DriveCam/Photos/yyyy-MM-dd

Temporary local files may be created while recording, uploading, retrying uploads, or playing videos. DriveCam attempts to delete temporary files after they are no longer needed, such as after a successful upload or cache cleanup.

DriveCam allows Android system backup and device-transfer features. Depending on your Android and Google backup settings, app-private preferences, local app database records, and other app-private data may be included in Android cloud backup or transferred to a new device. Media saved to Google Drive or user-selected folders remains governed by those storage locations and your account or device settings.

7. Sharing and Third Parties

DriveCam does not sell personal information. DriveCam does not share personal information with third-party advertisers.

Information may be processed or shared in the following limited cases:

  • Google services: Google Sign-In and Google Drive are used when you choose Drive features.
  • Device operating system: Android permission and storage systems process permission prompts, file access, notifications, and media handling.
  • Legal and safety reasons: information may be disclosed if required by law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.
  • Business transfer: if the app or developer business is transferred, information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to applicable law.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

  • Local recordings: remain on your device or selected storage folder until you delete them or app features delete temporary files.
  • Drive recordings: remain in your Google Drive until you delete them, revoke app access, or use DriveCam deletion scheduling features.
  • Upload history: may be stored locally in the app and can be cleared from the app where the feature is available.
  • Deletion schedules and history: may be stored in DriveCam-managed files or folders so DriveCam can track scheduled cleanup of DriveCam folders.
  • Playback cache: video playback cache may be deleted automatically after inactivity or during cache cleanup.
  • Account information: remains available while you are signed in. You can sign out in the app to disconnect the current Google account from DriveCam.

You can also manage or delete files directly in your Google Drive or device storage. If you uninstall DriveCam, files already saved outside the app's private storage, including files in Google Drive or selected folders, may remain until you delete them.

9. Security

DriveCam uses platform permission controls and Google authentication mechanisms to protect access to user data. Data sent to Google services is transmitted using secure network connections provided by Google APIs and the Android platform. No app can guarantee absolute security, but DriveCam is designed to limit data access to the features selected by the user.

10. Your Choices and Controls

  • You can deny or revoke camera, microphone, location, notification, or storage permissions in device settings.
  • You can choose whether recordings are saved to Google Drive or internal/local storage.
  • You can sign out of Google Drive from the app.
  • You can remove DriveCam's Google account access from your Google Account permissions page.
  • You can delete local recordings from the device or selected folder.
  • You can delete Drive recordings from Google Drive or use DriveCam deletion features where available.
  • You can clear visible upload history in the app where the feature is available.
